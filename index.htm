Medicine Guide

Pain & Fever Relief
expand_more

Paracetamol 500mg (Crocin)

Fever, headache, body pain. Max 4g/day for adults. Take after food.

Dolo 650mg

Paracetamol-based anti-pyretic. Safe for most patients. Do not combine with other Paracetamol products.

Ibuprofen 400mg (Brufen)

NSAID for inflammation, muscle pain, dental pain, menstrual cramps. Always take after meals.

Diclofenac 50mg (Voveran)

Severe pain, joint pain, arthritis, sprains. Take after food. Not for prolonged use.

Aspirin 75mg / 300mg

75mg daily for heart protection. 300mg chewable in suspected heart attack emergency.

Combiflam (Ibuprofen + Paracetamol)

Strong pain relief combo. Not for long-term use. Max 3 tablets/day.

Allergy & Antihistamines
expand_more

Cetirizine 10mg (Okacet)

Runny nose, hives, itching. May cause drowsiness. Take at night.

Loratadine 10mg (Claritin)

Non-drowsy antihistamine for seasonal allergies. Daytime use.

Levocetirizine 5mg

Stronger, non-drowsy alternative. For chronic urticaria and allergic rhinitis.

Hydrocortisone Cream 1%

Topical for skin allergies, insect bites, rashes. Apply 2-3 times daily.

Antibiotics
expand_more
Warning: Prescription required. Complete full course to prevent resistance.

Amoxicillin 500mg

Broad-spectrum. Throat, ear, skin infections. 5-7 day course.

Azithromycin 500mg

Respiratory and skin infections. Once daily for 3-5 days.

Ciprofloxacin 500mg

UTI, GI infections, respiratory. Empty stomach. Avoid dairy.

Metronidazole 400mg

Dental infections, amoebic dysentery. Strictly avoid alcohol.

Doxycycline 100mg

Broad-spectrum, malaria prophylaxis, acne. Avoid sun exposure. Not for children under 8.

Cardiac & Blood Pressure
expand_more

Amlodipine 5mg

Calcium channel blocker for hypertension. Same time daily.

Telmisartan 40mg

ARB for blood pressure. 24 hr coverage. Take with or without food.

Atenolol 50mg

Beta-blocker for hypertension, angina, post-heart attack.

Nitroglycerin 2.6mg (Sorbitrate)

Sublingual for chest pain. Keep under tongue. Repeat after 5 min if needed.

Emergency: Chest pain + sweating + left arm pain = Call 108 immediately.
GI & Digestive
expand_more

Omeprazole 20mg

Acid reflux, GERD, ulcer. Take 30 min before breakfast.

Pantoprazole 40mg

Stronger acid suppression for severe GERD. Take empty stomach.

Domperidone 10mg

Nausea, vomiting, bloating. Take 15 min before meals.

Ondansetron 4mg

Severe nausea/vomiting. Motion sickness, post-surgical nausea.

Loperamide 2mg

Acute diarrhea control. Stay hydrated with ORS alongside.

Respiratory & Asthma
expand_more

Salbutamol Inhaler (Asthalin)

Quick relief for asthma, wheezing. 2 puffs as needed.

Budesonide Inhaler

Maintenance inhaler. Reduces airway inflammation. Use daily.

Montelukast 10mg

Nighttime asthma control. Take before bed.

Dextromethorphan Syrup

Dry cough suppressant. Not for productive cough.

Breathing difficulty persists after inhaler = Call emergency immediately.
Diabetes Management
expand_more

Metformin 500mg

First-line Type 2 diabetes. Take with meals to reduce GI side effects.

Glimepiride 1-2mg

Stimulates insulin release. Watch for hypoglycemia (sweating, shaking).

Glucose D Tablets

For hypoglycemic episodes. 15g glucose, wait 15 min, recheck.

Normal Blood Sugar Range

Fasting: 70-100 mg/dL. Post-meal: under 140 mg/dL. Below 70 = Hypoglycemia.

Unconsciousness + diabetes history = Apply glucose gel, call 108.

Vitamin B-Complex

Nerve health, energy metabolism, deficiency recovery. After meals.

Iron (Ferrous Sulfate) 200mg

Calcium + Vitamin D3

Vitamin C 500mg

Immunity boost, wound healing, iron absorption aid.

Immunity, wound healing, cold recovery. Take with food.

Topical & External
expand_more

Betadine Solution

Wound cleaning, pre-surgical prep. Dilute for large wounds.

Clotrimazole Cream 1%

Fungal infections - ringworm, athlete's foot. Apply 2x daily for 2-4 weeks.

Silver Sulfadiazine Cream

First/second-degree burns. Apply sterile dressing over cream.

Moov / Volini Gel

Muscle pain, sprains, joint pain. Massage gently 2-3 times daily.

ORS & Rehydration
expand_more

ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts)

Dehydration from diarrhea, heat stroke, vomiting. Dissolve in 1L clean water.

Electral Powder

Electrolyte restoration. Better than plain water for dehydration recovery.

Glucose D Powder

Instant energy restoration. Mix in water. Useful for hypoglycemia.

Severe dehydration signs: dry mouth, no urine for 8hrs, sunken eyes = Call 108.
Emergency Medicines (Critical Use Only)
expand_more
These medicines are for emergency situations only. Always call 108 first.

Aspirin 300mg

Chew immediately in suspected heart attack (if no bleeding risk).

Nitroglycerin 2.6mg

Sublingual for chest pain. Keep under tongue. Call 108.

Glucose D / Sugar

For hypoglycemic unconsciousness. Apply on gums or under tongue.

Activated Charcoal

Poisoning/overdose first aid. Absorbs toxins in stomach. Call 108 first.

Epinephrine Auto-Injector

Anaphylaxis. Inject into outer thigh. Call 108. Keep patient legs elevated.

Tetanus Toxoid Injection

After any deep wound, bite, or dirty injury. Get within 48 hours.

First Aid Protocols

Priority Alpha

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

01. Check

Ensure the area is safe. Tap shoulder and shout to check for response. Check breathing.

02. Call

Dial 102 immediately. Ask someone to find an AED if available. Put phone on speaker.

03. Compress

Push hard and fast in center of chest (100-120 bpm) at 2 inches depth. 30 compressions, 2 breaths.

Continue until paramedics arrive or the person shows signs of life.

bloodtype Heavy Bleeding Control
expand_more

Step 1: Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage. Maintain firm pressure.

Step 2: If soaked through, add another layer—do NOT remove the first one.

Step 3: Elevate the limb above heart level if no fracture is suspected.

Tourniquet: Use only as a last resort for life-threatening limb bleeding. Note the time applied.

Call 108 if bleeding doesn't stop within 10 minutes.

whatshot Burns Treatment
expand_more

Step 1: Cool the burn under running cool (not cold) water for 10-15 minutes.

Step 2: Remove jewelry or tight clothing near the burn before swelling starts.

Step 3: Apply Silver Sulfadiazine cream. Cover with sterile non-stick dressing.

Avoid: Ice directly, butter, toothpaste, or bursting blisters.

Emergency: Burns larger than palm, on face/hands/joints, or chemical/electrical burns = Call 108.

air Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)
expand_more

Step 1: Stand behind the person and wrap arms around waist.

Step 2: Make a fist and place thumb side just above the navel, below ribcage.

Step 3: Grasp fist with other hand and perform quick upward thrusts.

Step 4: Repeat until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious.

If unconscious: Lower to ground, call 108, begin CPR immediately.

psychology Seizure Management
expand_more

Step 1: Ease the person to the floor. Turn them gently onto one side.

Step 2: Clear the area of hard or sharp objects. Place something soft under head.

Step 3: Remove glasses, loosen tight clothing around neck.

Critical: Do NOT put anything in their mouth. Do NOT restrain movements.

Call 108 if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes or repeats without recovery.

bone Fracture / Broken Bone
expand_more

Step 1: Do NOT move the injured limb. Keep it in the position found.

Step 2: Immobilize with a splint (wooden board, rolled newspaper, pillow).

Step 3: Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth for 20 minutes to reduce swelling.

Open Fracture: Cover wound with clean cloth. Do NOT push bone back in.

Call 108 for spine, hip, or skull suspected fractures. Do not move patient.

sunny Heat Stroke
expand_more

Step 1: Move to a cool, shaded area immediately. Remove excess clothing.

Step 2: Apply cool water to skin. Use fans or ice packs on armpits, groin, and neck.

Step 3: Give small sips of water or ORS only if conscious and alert.

Signs: Hot dry skin, confusion, rapid pulse, temperature above 103°F.

Call 108 if person is unconscious, confused, or vomiting.

skull Poisoning Response
expand_more

Step 1: Identify what was taken and how much. Check the container.

Step 2: Call Poison Control or 108 immediately. Keep the bottle for paramedics.

Do NOT: Induce vomiting unless instructed by a professional.

If unconscious: Place in recovery position (on side). Monitor breathing.

Activated Charcoal: Can be given if available. Absorbs toxins in stomach.

pest_control Snake Bite
expand_more

Step 1: Keep the patient calm. Panic increases venom spread.

Step 2: Immobilize the bitten limb. Keep it below heart level.

Step 3: Remove rings, watches, tight clothing before swelling starts.

Avoid: Cutting the wound, sucking venom, applying ice, or tourniquet.

Call 108 immediately. Note the snake's appearance if possible.

bolt Electric Shock
expand_more

Step 1: Turn off the power source immediately. Do NOT touch the person directly.

Step 2: Use a non-conductive object (wood, plastic) to separate person from source.

Step 3: Check breathing and pulse. Begin CPR if not breathing.

Step 4: Cover burns with sterile non-stick dressing. Do NOT apply ointment.

Call 108 for any electric shock injury. Internal damage may not be visible.

pool Drowning Rescue
expand_more

Step 1: Reach, throw, row—don't go in water unless trained. Use a rope, pole, or float.

Step 2: Once out, check breathing. If not breathing, start CPR immediately.

Step 3: Give 2 rescue breaths first, then 30 compressions. Water in lungs needs oxygen.

Step 4: If vomiting, turn head to side. Clear airway. Continue CPR.

Call 108 even if person seems fine. Secondary drowning can occur hours later.

water_drop Nosebleed
expand_more

Step 1: Sit upright and lean FORWARD (not backward). Pinch soft part of nose.

Step 2: Hold pressure continuously for 10-15 minutes. Breathe through mouth.

Step 3: Apply cold compress or ice pack to bridge of nose.

Avoid: Tilting head back, stuffing tissue in nose, or lying down.

Call 108 if bleeding lasts over 20 minutes or is from head injury.

sensor_occupied Fainting / Unconsciousness
expand_more

Step 1: Lay the person flat on their back. Elevate legs 12 inches above heart.

Step 2: Loosen tight clothing. Ensure fresh air circulation.

Step 3: If they vomit, turn them onto their side (recovery position).

Step 4: Do NOT give food or water until fully conscious and alert.

Call 108 if they don't regain consciousness within 1 minute or if breathing is abnormal.

visibility Eye Injury
expand_more

Chemical splash: Flush eye with clean water for 15 minutes. Hold eyelids open.

Foreign object: Do NOT rub the eye. Try flushing with saline or clean water.

Blunt trauma: Apply cold compress gently. Do NOT apply pressure to the eyeball.

Penetrating injury: Do NOT remove the object. Stabilize with a cup over the eye.

Call 108 for any chemical exposure, penetrating injury, or sudden vision loss.

pets Animal Bite
expand_more

Step 1: Wash the wound thoroughly with soap and running water for 10 minutes.

Step 2: Apply Betadine or antiseptic. Cover with clean sterile bandage.

Step 3: Get a Tetanus Toxoid injection within 48 hours if not up to date.

Rabies risk: For any dog/cat/wildlife bite, start rabies vaccine series immediately.

Call 108 if bite is deep, bleeding heavily, on face/hands, or from unknown animal.

directions_walk Sprain & Strain (RICE)
expand_more

R - Rest: Stop activity immediately. Do not bear weight on injured area.

I - Ice: Apply ice pack wrapped in cloth for 20 minutes every 2-3 hours. First 48 hours only.

C - Compression: Wrap with elastic bandage. Not too tight. Check circulation.

E - Elevation: Raise injured limb above heart level to reduce swelling.

Pain relief: Ibuprofen 400mg after food. After 48 hours, switch to warm compress.

Emergency Scenarios

local_fire_department Fire Safety Protocols
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Stay low to the floor to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Touch doors with the back of your hand before opening.
  • Use stairs, never the elevator.
  • Stop, Drop, and Roll if your clothes catch fire.
  • Call 101 from a safe location outside the building.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't hide in closets or under beds.
  • Don't go back for belongings once outside.
  • Don't open a door that feels hot to the touch.
  • Don't use water on electrical or grease fires.
landscape Earthquake Response
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On under sturdy furniture.
  • Move away from glass, windows, and exterior walls.
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings/wires.
  • Check for gas leaks and turn off main valve if you smell gas.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't run outside while the ground is still shaking.
  • Don't stand in a doorway; it's not the safest spot.
  • Don't use elevators after an earthquake.
  • Don't light matches—there may be gas leaks.
power_off Power Outages
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed.
  • Unplug sensitive electronics to avoid surge damage.
  • Use flashlights instead of candles to prevent fire risk.
  • Check on elderly neighbors and those on medical equipment.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't use a charcoal grill or gas oven indoors for heat.
  • Don't open the freezer more than necessary.
  • Don't touch downed power lines—call 1912 immediately.
water Flood Emergency
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Move to higher ground immediately. Do not wait.
  • Turn off electricity, gas, and water mains if safe.
  • Keep important documents in waterproof bags.
  • Boil all drinking water or use bottled water only.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't walk through moving water—6 inches can knock you down.
  • Don't drive through flooded roads. Turn around, don't drown.
  • Don't touch electrical equipment if wet.
cardiology Heart Attack Signs & Response
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Call 108 immediately. Every minute counts.
  • Chew 300mg Aspirin if available and no allergy.
  • Keep the person sitting in a comfortable position.
  • Loosen tight clothing. Stay calm and reassure them.
  • Be ready to perform CPR if they become unconscious.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't let the person deny symptoms or refuse help.
  • Don't give them food or drink (except Aspirin).
  • Don't drive them yourself—wait for paramedics.
  • Don't leave them alone even for a moment.
neurology Stroke Recognition (FAST)
expand_more
check_circle F.A.S.T. Check
  • Face: Ask them to smile. Does one side droop?
  • Arms: Ask them to raise both. Does one drift down?
  • Speech: Ask them to repeat a phrase. Is it slurred?
  • Time: If any sign is present, call 108 immediately.
  • Note the exact time symptoms started.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't wait to see if symptoms go away.
  • Don't give them food, drink, or medication.
  • Don't let them drive themselves to hospital.
  • Don't ignore symptoms because they seem mild.
allergy Severe Allergic Reaction
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Call 108 immediately. Anaphylaxis can be fatal in minutes.
  • Use Epinephrine auto-injector if available (outer thigh).
  • Lay person flat with legs elevated.
  • If vomiting or having trouble breathing, turn to side.
  • Remove the allergen (stinger, food, medication) if possible.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't wait to see if it gets better—it won't without treatment.
  • Don't give oral medications if they have trouble swallowing.
  • Don't let them sit up or stand if feeling faint.
monitor_heart Diabetic Emergency
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • If conscious and able to swallow, give fast-acting sugar (juice, candy, glucose gel).
  • Recheck after 15 minutes. Repeat if still low.
  • If unconscious, apply glucose gel to gums. Do NOT pour liquid.
  • Call 108 if person does not improve or loses consciousness.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't give food or drink to an unconscious person.
  • Don't give insulin—that could make it worse.
  • Don't leave them alone during an episode.
hazard Chemical Exposure
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Move away from the source immediately. Get to fresh air.
  • Remove contaminated clothing. Flush skin with water for 15 minutes.
  • For eye exposure: flush with clean water for 15 minutes, hold eyelids open.
  • Call 108. Bring the chemical container or SDS sheet.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't try to clean up a large chemical spill yourself.
  • Don't mix chemicals to neutralize them.
  • Don't breathe in fumes or vapors.
co2 Carbon Monoxide Poisoning
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Get everyone outside immediately into fresh air.
  • Call 108 and the gas company from outside.
  • Do NOT re-enter until professionals clear the building.
  • Watch for headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion—classic CO signs.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't stay inside to "figure out" the source.
  • Don't use generators, grills, or heaters indoors.
  • Don't ignore CO detector alarms.
mental_health Panic Attack Response
expand_more
check_circle DO THIS
  • Stay with the person. Speak calmly and reassuringly.
  • Guide them through slow breathing: 4 counts in, hold 4, out 6.
  • Move to a quiet, calm environment if possible.
  • Remind them the feeling will pass. It's temporary.
cancel AVOID THIS
  • Don't dismiss their feelings or tell them to "just calm down."
  • Don't crowd them or create more stimulation.
  • Don't leave them alone if they're severely distressed.
